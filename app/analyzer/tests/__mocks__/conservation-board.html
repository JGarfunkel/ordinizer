Mission Statement

The New Castle Conservation Board advises and makes recommendations to the Town Board and other Town departments, boards, and commissions regarding 1) proposed initiatives that impact the Town’s ecosystem, 2) open space and the preservation of our natural, scenic resources, and unique woodland and wetland ecosystems within the Town of New Castle, 3) protecting and enhancing habitat for our diverse plant and wildlife species, and 4) promoting the ecological health and biodiversity of the trees and other natural areas within the Town of New Castle.

The Conservation Board educates the public about the importance of responsible land stewardship - advocating for environmentally sound land management practices and utilization of native plantings on both Town and private properties.

The Conservation Board also has the authority to allocate and disburse funds for projects at Glazier Preserve financed through the Glazier Preserve Trust Account, subject to Town Board approval.

Establishment & Term of Office

The Conservation Board was established on May 13, 1975. The term of office is two years.
